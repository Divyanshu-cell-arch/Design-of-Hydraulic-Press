# Force & Cylinder Calculations

## Objective

To determine the hydraulic cylinder diameter required to generate a **50 kN** pressing force.

---

## Given

- Required Pressing Force, **F** = **50 kN = 50,000 N**
- Working Pressure, **P** = **160 bar = 16 MPa = 16 × 10⁶ Pa**

---

## Basic Relation

\[
F = P \times A
\]

Rearranging the equation to calculate the piston cross-sectional area:

\[
A = \frac{F}{P}
\]

---

## Area Calculation

\[
A = \frac{50,000}{16 \times 10^6}
\]

\[
A = 3.125 \times 10^{-3}\;m^2
\]

\[
A = 3125\;mm^2
\]

---

## Piston Diameter Calculation

The area of a circular piston is given by:

\[
A=\frac{\pi D^2}{4}
\]

Rearranging,

\[
D^2=\frac{4A}{\pi}
\]

Substituting the calculated area,

\[
D^2=\frac{4\times3125}{3.1416}
\]

\[
D^2\approx3976.07
\]

\[
D=\sqrt{3976.07}
\]

\[
D\approx63.05\;mm
\]

---

## Final Selection

- **Calculated piston diameter:** **63.05 mm**
- **Selected standard piston diameter:** **65 mm**

The standard diameter of **65 mm** was selected to provide an additional safety margin and ensure compatibility with commercially available hydraulic cylinders.

---

## Justification for 160 bar Working Pressure

A working pressure of **160 bar** is commonly used in industrial hydraulic systems as it provides an effective balance between force output, component strength, system reliability, and safety. Operating at this pressure allows the required **50 kN** pressing force to be achieved with a reasonably sized piston while avoiding excessive material thickness and unnecessary manufacturing costs.
